Welcome to the official Fizen Foundation whitepaper. Whether you are a token holder evaluating the protocol, a developer building on top of Fizen, a community participant shaping governance, or a researcher studying decentralized systems — this document is written for you.

Fizen is a decentralized protocol foundation. It exists to design, maintain, and grow an open protocol that no single company, team, or authority controls. The foundation's mandate is not to run a product but to steward a shared infrastructure: one governed by its community, funded by its ecosystem, and built for long-term sustainability.

## What Fizen is

Fizen is a protocol-layer foundation operating under the principle that critical infrastructure should be open, verifiable, and collectively owned. The Fizen protocol provides:

- **A coordination layer** for participants across the ecosystem — developers, node operators, token holders, and governance delegates
- **A transparent governance system** through which protocol upgrades and treasury allocations are proposed, debated, and ratified on-chain
- **A token model** (FIZEN) that aligns incentives between contributors, users, and the long-term health of the network
- **An open standard** that any application, integrator, or institution can build on without requiring permission

<Info>
  The Fizen Foundation does not operate as a company providing a service. It operates as a stewarding body that coordinates the protocol's evolution on behalf of its community.
</Info>
