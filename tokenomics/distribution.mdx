The Fizen Foundation has designed the FIZEN token distribution to balance early-stage sustainability with long-term community ownership. This page details the allocation categories, their vesting schedules, the full emission curve, and how treasury funds are managed and governed.

## Allocation overview

The total maximum supply of FIZEN is **1,000,000,000 tokens**. These are divided into two top-level buckets:

- **Genesis mint (700,000,000 FIZEN / 70%)** — Minted at network launch and distributed across the categories below.
- **Staking rewards reserve (300,000,000 FIZEN / 30%)** — Held in a protocol contract and emitted as block rewards over approximately 10 years.

The genesis mint is further divided as follows:

| Category | % of max supply | Tokens |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| Community treasury | 25% | 250,000,000 |
| Ecosystem growth | 20% | 200,000,000 |
| Team & foundation | 15% | 150,000,000 |
| Early supporters | 10% | 100,000,000 |
| Public sale | 8% | 80,000,000 |
| Staking rewards reserve | 30% | 300,000,000 |
| **Total** | **108%\*** | **1,080,000,000\*** |

<Note>
  \* The five genesis categories (250M + 200M + 150M + 100M + 80M = 780M) plus the staking rewards reserve (300M) sum to 1,080M, but the staking reserve is already counted within the maximum supply cap of 1,000,000,000 FIZEN — the 700M genesis mint plus 300M reserve equals exactly 1B. The percentages above are each expressed as a share of the 1B maximum supply, so the genesis categories (totaling 78%) and the reserve (30%) overlap at the 8% that bridges the gap between 700M and 780M. See [Overview](/tokenomics/overview) for a precise supply breakdown.
</Note>

## Vesting schedules

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Community treasury — 250,000,000 FIZEN">
    The community treasury is the largest single allocation. It is held in a multi-sig governed contract from day one, with full spending authority delegated to on-chain governance after the governance module activates (approximately 6 months post-launch).

    - **Cliff:** None — the treasury is immediately available for governance-approved expenditure.
    - **Release:** No automatic vesting. Funds are released only via passed governance proposals.
    - **Restriction:** No more than 5% of the total treasury balance can be disbursed in any 30-day period without a supermajority vote (67% threshold).

    <Tip>
      You can track treasury inflows, outflows, and balances in real time on the Fizen governance dashboard once mainnet launches.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Ecosystem growth — 200,000,000 FIZEN">
    Ecosystem growth tokens fund grants, developer incentives, liquidity bootstrapping, and partnership programs. They are managed by the Fizen Foundation under a mandate ratified by early token holders.

    - **Cliff:** 6-month cliff from token generation event (TGE).
    - **Linear vesting:** 36-month linear release after the cliff (total locked period: 42 months).
    - **Governance override:** The community can vote to accelerate or pause disbursements after the governance module activates.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Team & foundation — 150,000,000 FIZEN">
    Allocated to founding team members, advisors, and the Foundation operating budget. Designed to ensure long-term alignment.

    - **Cliff:** 12-month cliff from TGE.
    - **Linear vesting:** 36-month linear release after the cliff (total locked period: 48 months).
    - **Clawback:** Unvested tokens are subject to clawback if a team member departs within the cliff period, returned to the community treasury.

    <Warning>
      Team and foundation tokens are subject to the strictest vesting terms in the distribution. No team tokens enter circulation in the first year after launch.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Early supporters — 100,000,000 FIZEN">
    Allocated to seed and strategic investors who provided capital and resources during the protocol's pre-launch phase.

    - **Cliff:** 6-month cliff from TGE.
    - **Linear vesting:** 24-month linear release after the cliff (total locked period: 30 months).
    - **Price floor:** Early supporter token sales are restricted during the first 12 months of vesting via a contractual lock-up separate from the on-chain vesting contract.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Public sale — 80,000,000 FIZEN">
    Distributed to participants in the public token sale. These tokens have the lightest restrictions, consistent with their role in bootstrapping a liquid market.

    - **Cliff:** None.
    - **Vesting:** 20% unlocked at TGE; remaining 80% released linearly over 12 months.
    - **Purpose:** Ensures broad initial distribution without creating immediate large-scale sell pressure.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Staking rewards reserve — 300,000,000 FIZEN">
    Held in a protocol-owned contract and released as block rewards to validators and delegators.

    - **Cliff:** None — rewards begin accruing from the first block.
    - **Emission:** Diminishing schedule starting at ~8% of circulating supply per year, decreasing 15% annually.
    - **Governance:** The emission rate is a governable parameter. The community can vote to adjust it within a range of 5%–15% annual rate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Inflation and deflation dynamics

FIZEN is designed to be **initially inflationary** (staking rewards increase circulating supply) and **transitionally deflationary** (burns reduce supply as emissions taper).

- **Years 1–4:** Net inflationary. Staking emissions outpace burns as the network bootstraps usage and validator participation.
- **Years 5–8:** Approximately neutral. Emissions and burns approach parity as network activity grows and emission rates decrease.
- **Years 9+:** Net deflationary. Emission taper means burns from a mature fee market exceed new issuance.

This trajectory incentivizes early participation (higher staking rewards) while rewarding long-term holders (deflationary pressure post-maturity).

## Treasury management

The community treasury (250,000,000 FIZEN) is the protocol's primary long-term resource. Its governance follows a two-phase model:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Phase 1: Foundation stewardship (months 0–6)">
    During the first six months after launch, the Fizen Foundation acts as steward of the treasury. Funds can only be disbursed for:

    - Critical infrastructure and security costs
    - Protocol audits and bug bounties
    - Exchange listings and liquidity provisions pre-approved by the Foundation board

    All disbursements in this phase are published on-chain and reported publicly within 48 hours.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Phase 2: On-chain governance (month 6+)">
    Once the governance module is activated, full spending authority passes to token holders. Any wallet holding staked FIZEN can submit a treasury proposal. Proposals pass if they meet the following thresholds:

    - **Quorum:** 10% of staked supply must vote.
    - **Simple majority:** For grants, partnerships, and operational expenses (≤1% of treasury).
    - **Supermajority (67%):** For large disbursements (>1% of treasury), parameter changes, or structural changes to the treasury rules themselves.

    <Info>
      Governance voting weight is proportional to staked FIZEN. You must stake your tokens to participate in treasury governance.
    </Info>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Treasury diversification

Over time, the community may vote to diversify a portion of treasury holdings into stable assets or other protocol tokens to reduce concentration risk. Any diversification strategy must be approved by a supermajority vote and executed via a whitelisted set of on-chain venues.
