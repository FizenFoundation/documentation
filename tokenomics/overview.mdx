The FIZEN token is the native asset of the Fizen Foundation protocol. It is not incidental to the system — it is the mechanism through which network security, on-chain governance, and economic coordination are made possible. This page introduces the token, its supply model, and why it is structurally necessary for the protocol to operate.

## What is FIZEN?

FIZEN is a programmable protocol token deployed on the Fizen network. It serves three interdependent functions:

1. **Network security** — Token holders stake FIZEN to participate in consensus and earn rewards. The economic stake they put at risk is what makes attacking the network prohibitively expensive.
2. **Protocol fees** — All transactions and smart contract interactions on the Fizen network are denominated in FIZEN. A portion of each fee is burned, creating long-term deflationary pressure.
3. **Governance** — Staked FIZEN confers voting rights over protocol upgrades, parameter changes, and treasury allocations.

These three roles are tightly coupled. Removing any one of them would undermine the others: governance without stake has no economic weight; fees without a native token cannot be burned; staking without governance yields no meaningful control.

## Why a token is necessary

<Info>
  This section explains the reasoning behind the token design. If you are primarily interested in allocation or mechanics, you can skip ahead to [Distribution](/tokenomics/distribution) or [Utility](/tokenomics/utility).
</Info>

A dedicated token is necessary for several reasons:

- **Sybil resistance** — Governance and consensus mechanisms must be expensive to game. A freely acquirable resource cannot provide this property. FIZEN's market price and finite supply create the cost floor required.
- **Aligned incentives** — When the people who operate the network hold the token, their financial interests are tied to the health of the protocol. A token-less system lacks this alignment.
- **Autonomous fee markets** — A self-contained fee market — where block space is priced in the native token and fees are redistributed to validators and burned — allows the protocol to operate without relying on an external currency or off-chain payment system.
- **Permissionless participation** — Any holder can stake, vote, or pay fees without seeking permission from any central party. The token is the credential.

## Total supply and issuance model

FIZEN has a fixed maximum supply of **1,000,000,000 (one billion)** tokens. No new tokens can be minted beyond this cap; the protocol's smart contracts enforce this at the consensus layer.

At genesis, **700,000,000 FIZEN** (70% of the maximum supply) are minted and allocated according to the distribution schedule described in [Distribution](/tokenomics/distribution). The remaining **300,000,000 FIZEN** (30%) are reserved as staking rewards and are released on a diminishing emission schedule over approximately 10 years.

<Note>
  The staking reward reserve is not controlled by any single entity. It is held in a protocol-owned contract and released automatically according to on-chain parameters. The governance system can vote to adjust the emission rate within defined bounds.
</Note>

### Emission schedule

Staking rewards are emitted in epochs. The initial annual emission rate is approximately 8% of the circulating supply. This rate decreases by 15% each year until the reward reserve is exhausted or a governance vote modifies the schedule.

```text
Year 1:  ~80,000,000 FIZEN emitted  (~8% of genesis circulating supply)
Year 2:  ~68,000,000 FIZEN emitted
Year 3:  ~57,800,000 FIZEN emitted
...
Year 10: ~23,300,000 FIZEN emitted  (reserve approaches exhaustion)
```

<Tip>
  After the staking reward reserve is fully emitted, the protocol transitions to a fee-sustaining model. Validators are compensated entirely from transaction fees, supplemented by any governance-approved treasury grants.
</Tip>

### Burn mechanics

A portion of every transaction fee is permanently burned. The default burn rate is **20% of the base fee** per transaction. This rate is a governable parameter, with a minimum floor of 10% and a maximum ceiling of 50% to prevent governance attacks that could make the token deflationary to the point of being unusable for fees.

Over time, burns create deflationary pressure that offsets issuance from the staking reward reserve, and eventually — as emissions taper — reduces circulating supply below the genesis allocation.

## Summary

FIZEN is a fixed-supply token that fuels every layer of the Fizen protocol — from paying for computation to securing consensus to governing the protocol's future. Its design reflects a deliberate choice to make participation economically meaningful: holding, staking, and using FIZEN all carry real cost and real benefit.
