The Fizen ecosystem grows through collaboration. The foundation partners with organizations that share a commitment to open, decentralized infrastructure — from the validators and node operators who secure the network, to the application developers who build on top of it, to the exchanges and research institutions that support its growth.

This page explains the types of partnerships the foundation supports, what partnership means in practice, and how to apply.

## What partnership means

A partnership with the Fizen Foundation is a formal, documented relationship — not a marketing label. Partners are listed publicly, and the foundation takes responsibility for the integrations it endorses.

Partnership is built on three commitments:

1. **Technical alignment** — your infrastructure or application works correctly with the Fizen protocol and you maintain that compatibility as the protocol evolves.
2. **Values alignment** — you operate transparently, support open-source development, and do not act in ways that harm the network or its users.
3. **Active participation** — you engage with the Fizen community, provide feedback on protocol changes, and contribute to the ecosystem over time.

<Note>
  Partnership status does not imply endorsement of a partner's products, services, or financial instruments by the Fizen Foundation. The foundation does not provide investment advice.
</Note>

## Partnership benefits

The benefits available to partners depend on the type and depth of the relationship, but the foundation offers the following to all formal partners:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ecosystem grants">
    Partners building open-source infrastructure, tooling, or public goods may apply for grants from the Fizen ecosystem fund. Grant applications from existing partners receive expedited review. See the [Community](/ecosystem/community) page for grant details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Co-marketing and visibility">
    Partners are listed on the official Fizen ecosystem directory. The foundation may feature partner projects in community calls, newsletters, and social media. Joint announcements can be coordinated with the foundation's communications team.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Technical support">
    Partners receive direct access to the foundation's protocol engineering team for integration questions, architecture reviews, and issue escalation. This includes access to pre-release documentation and changelogs ahead of public announcements.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Early access">
    Partners are invited to participate in testnet phases, preview protocol upgrades before they go to governance vote, and provide feedback during the improvement proposal process. Infrastructure partners may join the validator candidate pool ahead of public applications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Governance participation">
    Partners are invited to participate in governance forums and community calls as stakeholders. While voting power on the Fizen protocol is always token-weighted, partner feedback is actively solicited during proposal drafting.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Integration support

The foundation's developer relations team provides structured support for integrations, regardless of formal partnership status. Integration support includes:

- **Technical documentation** — maintained guides for connecting to Fizen RPC endpoints, interacting with on-chain governance, and integrating the FIZEN token
- **SDK and library support** — official SDKs maintained by the foundation with versioned releases and migration guides
- **Architecture review** — for projects building significant infrastructure on Fizen, the engineering team offers design reviews before implementation begins
- **Testnet access** — all developers can access the Fizen testnet with test tokens via the public faucet; no partnership required

<Tip>
  If you are building on Fizen and have a technical question that is not covered in the documentation, post in the developer forum or open an issue on the relevant GitHub repository.
</Tip>

## How to apply

The partnership application process is designed to be straightforward. The foundation reviews applications on a rolling basis.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the partnership criteria">
    Read the technical and values alignment criteria above. Confirm that your organization meets the baseline requirements before applying.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare your application">
    Your application should describe your organization, the type of partnership you are seeking, how you use or plan to use the Fizen protocol, and what you are looking for from the foundation.

    For infrastructure partners, include details about your current infrastructure, geographic distribution, and uptime track record.

    For application developers, include a technical description of your project and links to any public repositories or deployments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit via the partner portal">
    Submit your application through the Fizen Foundation partner portal, accessible from the official Fizen Foundation website. The foundation will acknowledge receipt within five business days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Technical review">
    The foundation's developer relations team will review your application and may schedule a call to discuss your integration or ask clarifying questions. For infrastructure partners, this step includes a review of your validator or node setup.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Partnership agreement">
    Approved partners sign a partnership agreement that formalizes the relationship, outlines mutual commitments, and defines the specific benefits applicable to your partnership type.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  The foundation receives more applications than it can formally onboard at any time. If your application is not approved immediately, the team will provide feedback and may suggest reapplying when your project has reached a later stage.
</Info>
