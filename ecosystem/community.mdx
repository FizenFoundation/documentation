The Fizen Foundation is built to hand control to its community. That only works if the community is informed, engaged, and willing to participate. This page explains where the community lives, how you can contribute, and the programs the foundation runs to support contributors at every level.

## Developer grants program

The Fizen ecosystem fund allocates a portion of the treasury to grants for developers building open-source infrastructure, tooling, and applications on the Fizen protocol. Grants are awarded on a rolling basis, with cohort reviews every quarter.

### What is funded

- Core infrastructure: node software, clients, indexers, and explorers
- Developer tooling: SDKs, testing frameworks, local development environments
- Applications: protocols, dApps, and integrations that expand what is possible on Fizen
- Documentation and education: guides, tutorials, translations, and educational content
- Research: protocol research, cryptography, and formal verification

### Grant sizes

Grants are tiered by scope:

| Tier | Scope | Size |
|------|-------|------|
| Micro | Small improvements, documentation, minor tooling | Up to 5,000 FIZEN |
| Standard | New tooling, integrations, or small protocols | 5,000 – 50,000 FIZEN |
| Major | Significant infrastructure or multi-component systems | 50,000 – 250,000 FIZEN |
| Strategic | Critical ecosystem infrastructure, reviewed by governance | Above 250,000 FIZEN |

### How to apply

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check existing grants">
    Review the grants registry on the foundation website to see what has already been funded. The foundation avoids funding duplicate work — if something similar exists, consider building on it or coordinating with the existing grantee.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a proposal">
    Your grant proposal should include: a clear description of what you are building, why it matters to the Fizen ecosystem, your technical approach, a milestone-based timeline, your team's background, and a detailed budget breakdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit to the grants portal">
    Submit your proposal through the official grants portal on the Fizen Foundation website. Micro and standard grant applications are reviewed by the foundation's grants committee. Major and strategic grants go to a community governance vote.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and feedback">
    The grants committee will review your application and provide feedback within two to four weeks. You may be asked to revise your proposal or attend a review call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Milestone-based disbursement">
    Approved grants are paid in installments tied to milestones. You submit milestone reports, and the committee verifies completion before releasing the next tranche.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Before submitting a grant proposal, share a brief summary in the developer forum and gather feedback from the community. Proposals with demonstrated community support are more likely to be approved.
</Tip>

## Ambassador program

The Fizen Ambassador Program supports community members who want to grow Fizen's presence in their region, language community, or technical specialty area.

### What ambassadors do

- Represent Fizen at local meetups, conferences, and hackathons
- Translate documentation and announcements into their language
- Run local Fizen community chapters on Discord or Telegram
- Create educational content — blog posts, videos, workshops
- Provide on-the-ground feedback from their community to the foundation

### Local chapters

Any ambassador can establish a local chapter for their city, country, or language. Local chapters are listed on the official Fizen community directory and receive a channel in the main Discord server. Chapters are expected to follow the code of conduct and maintain active participation.

### How to apply

To apply for the ambassador program, post an introduction in the ambassador channel on the official Discord server or the community forum. Include your background, your community context, and what you plan to do as an ambassador. The foundation's community team reviews applications on a rolling basis.

<Info>
  Ambassadors are not employees of the Fizen Foundation. The program provides recognition, early access to announcements, and coordination support — not a salary. Exceptional contributors may be eligible for grants or bounties for specific deliverables.
</Info>

## Bug bounty program

Security is a precondition for everything else. The Fizen Foundation runs a continuous bug bounty program to incentivize responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities in the protocol, smart contracts, and official tooling.

### Scope

The bug bounty program covers:

- **In scope:** Fizen protocol node software, on-chain governance contracts, official bridge contracts, official SDKs, and the foundation website
- **Out of scope:** Third-party applications, unofficial forks, social engineering attacks, and theoretical vulnerabilities without a working proof of concept

### Severity and rewards

| Severity | Description | Reward range |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Critical | Remote code execution, asset theft, consensus failure | 50,000 – 500,000 FIZEN |
| High | Significant fund loss, privilege escalation, major denial of service | 10,000 – 50,000 FIZEN |
| Medium | Limited fund loss, partial DoS, data integrity issues | 1,000 – 10,000 FIZEN |
| Low | Minor information disclosure, non-critical bugs | 100 – 1,000 FIZEN |

### Responsible disclosure

Submit findings privately to the foundation's security team through the official security disclosure channel before publishing. The foundation commits to acknowledging your report within 48 hours and providing a status update within 10 business days. Researchers who follow responsible disclosure are publicly credited (with their consent) after the fix is deployed.

<Warning>
  Do not test against mainnet or testnet in ways that could disrupt other users. Run reproductions locally or in an isolated environment. Disruptive testing may disqualify a submission.
</Warning>

## Community calls and governance forums

### Community calls

The foundation hosts recurring community calls open to all participants. Calls typically cover:

- Protocol development updates from the core engineering team
- Ecosystem grant announcements and project spotlights
- Open Q&A with the foundation team
- Preview of upcoming governance proposals

Call schedules and recordings are posted to the official forum and announced across all community channels.

### Governance forums

Before any Fizen Improvement Proposal (FIP) goes to an on-chain vote, it goes through a structured deliberation process on the governance forum. This is where you can:

- Read and comment on proposals in their draft stage
- Ask questions of proposal authors
- Signal support or concern before the formal vote
- Propose amendments that improve a draft

See the [Governance](/governance/overview) section for a full explanation of how proposals move from idea to on-chain vote.

## Code of conduct

The Fizen community is built on openness and good faith. All community channels — Discord, Telegram, the forum, and community calls — operate under the following principles:

- **Respect:** Treat other community members with respect, even when you disagree with them.
- **Good faith:** Engage with arguments on their merits. Assume others are acting in good faith unless there is clear evidence otherwise.
- **No harassment:** Harassment, personal attacks, and threats of any kind are not tolerated.
- **No spam:** Do not spam channels with unsolicited promotions, especially of unrelated projects or financial schemes.
- **Constructive disagreement:** Disagreement is healthy. Attack ideas, not people. Provide reasons for your positions.
- **Honesty:** Do not misrepresent your identity, affiliations, or the nature of your project.

Violations of the code of conduct may result in removal from community channels. Serious or repeated violations may result in a permanent ban. Moderation decisions can be appealed to the foundation's community team.

<Note>
  The code of conduct applies to all official Fizen community spaces. Community members who feel it has been violated can report incidents through the moderation channel on the official Discord server.
</Note>
